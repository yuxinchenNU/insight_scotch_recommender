Yuxin Chen




I am doing my PhD in applied mathematics at Northwestern University under the supervision of Mary Silber, where my research focus was on bottom-up mechanistic modeling and I studied tipping points in earth systems (such as desertification in drylands and loss of Arctic sea ice) using dynamical systems, stochastic processes, and Monte Carlo methods.

In recent years, I have developed strong interests in top-down data-driven methods. I started to look at problems by combining mechanistic models with observed data (particle filter method and ensemble Kalman filter) when I visited The Statistical and Applied Mathematical Sciences Institute (SAMSI) in Spring 2018. In collaboration with John MacLean and Chris Jones, I worked on hurricane intensity forecasting by improving time-dependent parameter estimation in a physics-based model of hurricane system using observed and reanalysis data.

At Northwestern, I have worked on a project with Niall Mangan to infer infectious disease transmission direction using data-driven methods, including manifold learning and nonlinear time series analysis.

I am aiming to apply my quantitative skills to solve real life problems and to help with making this world a better place!
